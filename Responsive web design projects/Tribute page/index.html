A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

A brief memoir of Dr. Kalam's life:

1931

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the 'Missile Man of India', was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. His full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam.

1951

Kalam graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1958 as an aerospace engineer. He joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), where he made significant contributions to the development of India's space and missile programs.

1980

He became widely known for his work on the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully launched the Rohini satellite in 1980.

1998

In 1998, Kalam played a crucial role as the Chief Project Coordinator for Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which established India as a nuclear power.

2002

In 2002, Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the "People's President" due to his approachable and inspiring personality, particularly among the youth.

2015

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, after suffering a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He left behind a legacy of scientific innovation, patriotism, and commitment to education, making him one of India's most beloved and respected public figures.
